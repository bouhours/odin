# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free environment for everyone participating in this project.

We welcome contributors regardless of experience level, background, identity, nationality, language, disability, age, or perspective.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate.
- Communicate constructively.
- Give and receive feedback gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the project and its users.
- Acknowledge mistakes and learn from them.
- Respect maintainers' time and decisions.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes, but is not limited to:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory or derogatory language.
- Trolling, insults, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer guidance.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior.

Maintainers may take appropriate action in response to unacceptable behavior, including:

- A private or public warning.
- Temporary restriction from participating.
- Closing or locking issues and pull requests.
- Temporary or permanent ban from the project community.
- Reporting behavior to GitHub or other relevant platforms when appropriate.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the maintainers using the private reporting channel listed in the repository documentation or security policy.

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as possible. Maintainers will respect privacy and confidentiality where reasonable and appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, community chat, events, and any public representation of the project.
